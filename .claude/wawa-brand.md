## Materials & Construction

**Current method:**
- **Timber:** Paulownia — lightweight, water-resistant, grown along South Africa's eastern coast
- **Core:** Recycled/reclaimed foam blanks — stripped and reshaped
- **Rails:** Cork — durable, repairable, reduced petrochemical use
- **Reinforcement:** Hardwood nose and tail blocks
- **Finish:** Optional watercolor finishes reflecting ocean tones

**Original approach (early boards):**
Solid timber from locally harvested Erythrina, agave, cypress, stinkwood, and kapok

---

## Process

1. Reclaimed foam blanks stripped and reshaped
2. CNC cutting from digital blueprints developed through 15+ years of experimentation
3. Paulownia timber skins wrapped over core
4. Hand woodwork — cork rails, hardwood nose/tail detailing
5. Hand-finishing
6. Optional watercolor finish

## Copy Brief (approved)

### Home
Honouring surfing's heritage. Shaped for today.

Wawa wooden surfboards reconnect modern surfing with its origins. Inspired by the timber boards ridden by early Polynesian wave riders, each piece is crafted in Muizenberg, Cape Town using responsibly sourced wood and sustainable design principles.

*This is where tradition meets performance.*

### Boards
Timber. Balance. Flow.

Wawa boards draw inspiration from classic forms — alaias, paipos and refined contemporary shapes — reimagined through modern shaping techniques.

Each board features:
- Lightweight, water-resistant paulownia timber
- Recycled foam core construction
- Cork rails for durability and reduced petrochemical use
- Hand-finished detailing

*Wood behaves differently from conventional materials. It flexes with intention. It carries momentum. It creates a distinct connection between rider and wave.*

Every grain pattern is unique. No two boards are the same. Custom builds are shaped to suit your style, local conditions and experience level.

### Story
A return to origin.

Wawa was founded by Cobus Joubert, driven by a lifelong relationship with the ocean and a desire to build boards that feel purposeful.

Surfing's earliest boards were carved from timber. Over time, foam and fiberglass replaced wood in the pursuit of convenience and mass production. Wawa revisits that original material — not as nostalgia, but as evolution.

By combining traditional inspiration with modern precision shaping, the brand bridges past and present. Each board honours surfing's lineage while embracing contemporary performance.

### Craft
Built with intention.

Every Wawa board begins with responsibly sourced paulownia — chosen for its strength, lightness and natural resistance to water. Timber skins are wrapped over recycled cores to reduce waste while maintaining structural integrity. Cork rails provide resilience and allow for easier repair over time.

Precision CNC shaping ensures accuracy. Hand-finishing ensures character.

The result is a board that is durable, repairable and designed to age beautifully with sun, salt and use.

*Sustainability is embedded in the process — from materials to longevity.*

### Contact
Connect with the workshop.

Wawa boards are crafted in Muizenberg, Cape Town, South Africa. For custom orders, collaborations or general enquiries.

Production is intentionally limited to maintain quality and attention to detail.
